## 1. The verdict in one paragraph

**The SLVP ideal for fastest-safe adaptive collection is a single closed loop that controls one variable — the quota-unit consumption rate (the GCRA-paced inter-request interval), not raw request count — with conservative-increment AIMD dynamics whose additive step is slow enough that the controller never reaches its ceiling by surprise within one run; the ceiling itself is a hard, operator-set, behavioral-not-technical cap that the probe is forbidden to cross; the retry layer is structurally separate and never double-pays backoff already absorbed by a request's own wait; and any work the run cannot complete is deferred to durable gap records keyed to discriminate provider-pressure from voluntary caps.** Stated as a principle: *adapt rate downward fast and upward slow, inside a fixed safety envelope you never probe, and turn everything you couldn't fetch into a durable re-entry point instead of a faster probe.* **Confidence: 96% on the architecture** (one loop, one rate variable, AIMD-with-ceiling, separate retry, gap substrate — every production reference and all three adversarial verdicts converge here). **Confidence: ~80% on two sub-points** that the research could not settle to >95%: (a) whether a *latency / behavioral-challenge* early-warning signal is usable through the neko browser path or is too noisy to wire (Attack 3 — empirical, unmeasured), and (b) whether ChatGPT's 429s name their quota axis (RPM vs TPM) richly enough to make the rate variable *token-weighted* rather than request-weighted (Attack 1 — depends on opaque provider response bodies we have not characterized). The safety-ceiling *value* is deliberately excluded from the confidence claim — it is owner's-call, not a research output.

## 2. The minimal essential design

**The single control variable: quota-unit consumption rate, expressed as a GCRA-paced inter-request interval.** All five production references that face a quota-enforced single resource (AWS adaptive mode, Scrapy, the ATB algorithm) control *rate*, not concurrency. The Netflix/Envoy *concurrency* school applies only when the bottleneck is a downstream queue probed via latency — which does not exist for an account-level RPM/TPM quota. For PDPP the choice is forced (see fork below) and the variable is the inter-request interval held by `ProviderPacing._currentIntervalMs`.

**The signal: 429 as the definitive hard signal; behavioral/challenge signals as the early-warning class where observable.** 429 drives multiplicative decrease (`ProviderPacing.recordThrottle`). The research's BBR/Vegas lesson — *don't touch the ceiling to estimate it* — is honored not by a precise latency model (which the neko path likely can't deliver) but by (i) a conservative increment that keeps the operating point well below the ceiling and (ii) treating challenge-class signals (CAPTCHA, re-auth interstitial, empty-where-non-empty content) as *session-trust* events routed to the retry/health layer, not as ordinary backoff.

**The dynamics: conservative-increment AIMD.** Additive decrease of the interval on each clean success (`recordSuccess` → `interval -= additiveIncreaseMs`, floored at `minIntervalMs`); multiplicative *increase* of the interval on throttle (`recordThrottle` → `interval /= multiplicativeDecreaseFactor`, never below `initialIntervalMs`). Per Attack 2's amendment, the additive step must be small enough that a full recovery from the throttled interval back to the floor spans **multiple successful runs**, not multiple requests within one run — so the upward probe is observable over time windows and never lunges at the ceiling.

**The retry layer (separate).** `connector-http-governor`/`retryHttp` owns per-request re-attempts and Retry-After honoring; the rate loop owns inter-request pace. The seam that prevents double-paying is the `absorbedByRequestWait` flag (`adaptive-lane.ts:297`) and `retryAfterAlreadySlept` (`index.ts:2571`): when a request already slept its own backoff and then succeeded, the lane reduces pace and emits the event but does **not** add the same wait again to the next launch cooldown.

**The safety ceiling.** Two hard bounds the adaptive loop may never cross: `minIntervalMs` (the fastest interval AIMD can reach) and `maxConcurrency = 1` (in-flight parallelism, fixed by ban risk). These are not discovered; they are operator priors set *below the estimated behavioral flagging threshold*, not merely below the observed 429 threshold (Attack 2 + Frame 4).

**The gap-deferral substrate.** When a run stops early for any reason, the unfetched tail becomes durable `DETAIL_GAP` records. The reason code discriminates `upstream_pressure` (arms the cross-run scheduler cooldown) from `run_cap_deferred` (does not). This is the irreducible "lose nothing" guarantee and the cross-run analogue of multiplicative decrease.

**Diagram-in-prose of the one loop:**
> For each conversation-detail request: the single pre-flight gate waits `max(launchDelay, cooldown, pacingDelayHint())` — never a sum — where `pacingDelayHint()` is the GCRA interval the rate-AIMD currently owes. Fire the request through the (separate) retry layer. On success → `recordSuccess()` shaves the interval toward `minIntervalMs` (slow additive increase). On 429 → `recordThrottle()` multiplies the interval up and the lane drops to `minConcurrency`; if the request already slept its own Retry-After, skip the duplicate cooldown. If pressure accumulates past the density stop, or the run/wall budget trips, emit `DETAIL_GAP` for the remaining tail (reason-discriminated) and exit. Across runs, the scheduler cooldown reads only `upstream_pressure` gaps. One variable, one signal, one wait, one loop.

**The rate-vs-concurrency fork, resolved decisively for PDPP.** Decision rule from the research: *choose rate when the upstream enforces a load-independent quota signaled by 429/quota number; choose concurrency when you own or can latency-probe a downstream queue.* Applied to PDPP's facts — browser-mediated (neko), single consumer account, one provider, account-level RPM/TPM quota, in-flight parallelism hard-pinned at 1 by ban risk — **rate wins, unambiguously.** At `maxConcurrency = 1` the rate/concurrency plane collapses to a line (`rate = 1/interval`), so a concurrency-AIMD over a variable that can never exceed 1 is dead code. The latency signal that makes concurrency control attractive (Netflix/Envoy) is both absent (quota timer ignores in-flight count) and unobservable cleanly through the browser VM. Rate is the single correct variable; concurrency is a hard ceiling, not a controller.

## 3. Essential vs incidental, for PDPP specifically

**The job (Hickey-style essence):** "Collect a personal data source as fast as the provider *safely* allows, losing nothing." Three obligations — maximize throughput up to safe saturation, respond correctly when exceeded (no ban, no loss), preserve everything not fetched this run.

**KEEP — the irreducibles** (each maps directly to an obligation):
1. **One pre-flight rate gate, AIMD on interval.** Without runtime self-calibration you either throttle below the safe rate or trigger a ban. `ProviderPacing` + the lane's single `max(...)` wait *is* this — already converged in code (`adaptive-lane.ts:466`, `index.ts:456,2396`).
2. **429 congestion signal.** The only reliable saturation signal for a quota-enforced API; latency here is uninformative or noisy.
3. **Separate retry layer with Retry-After + `absorbedByRequestWait`.** Prevents double-gating and retry-storm amplification of the very signal that caused the failure.
4. **Hard safety ceiling (`minIntervalMs` floor + `maxConcurrency = 1`).** Ban is irreversible; the loop must be capped *below* the behavioral threshold the operator estimates.
5. **Durable `DETAIL_GAP` records.** The "lose nothing" guarantee for bounded runs.
6. **Cross-run scheduler cooldown discriminated by gap reason.** A capped run is not a saturated provider; arming the cooldown on `run_cap_deferred` would inject false inter-run delays. The *discrimination* is essential correctness, not scaffolding.

**DELETE — the incidental complexity** (each is derivable-as-wrong from the keep-list):
1. **The fixed launch-jitter floor `CONVO_DETAIL_PAUSE_MIN_MS = 1500` (and `MAX = 3000`)** wired in as the lane's `minDelayMs`/`maxDelayMs` (`index.ts:1733–1734, 2389–2392`). *This is the single biggest delete.* It is a constant masquerading as control. Because the lane waits `max(launchDelay, cooldown, hint)`, this floor **overrides the GCRA rate-AIMD from below**: the rate loop can never probe an interval shorter than 1500ms even when the provider would accept it, capping throughput at ~19–40 conv/min regardless of what `ProviderPacing` learns. Jitter is a *retry-path desynchronization* technique for multiple competing flows (Frame 1 Point 7); a single-client serial collector has no competing flows, so a fixed jitter *floor* has no convergence role — it is a hand-tuned manual throttle from the 2026-06-02 probe. The AIMD's own `minIntervalMs` is the principled floor; this constant should drop to a sub-second pattern-avoidance jitter (or zero) so the rate loop becomes the binding governor.
2. **Frozen concurrency-AIMD.** `maxConcurrency = 1` (`index.ts:1735–1736`) makes `maybeIncreaseConcurrency`/`decreaseConcurrency` (`adaptive-lane.ts:258,310`) dead code — `currentConcurrency >= maxConcurrency` is always true. Either the lane keeps concurrency as a *non-adaptive serial constraint* (correct for PDPP) and the AIMD-on-concurrency machinery is acknowledged as inert, or it's removed. It is not a second control dimension; the fork settled that.
3. **The tailStop self-terminating recovery exit (`AbortController` after gap emission, `index.ts:599`).** This optimization exists *only because* the 1500ms floor makes iterating a hundreds-long tail (each emitting just a gap record, no I/O value) cost minutes. Delete the floor and the tail iterates at sub-millisecond overhead; the abort becomes a micro-optimization, not a required mechanism. It is a downstream consequence of delete #1 being wrong.
4. **Dual pressure detectors as separate mechanisms** — the `RetryExhaustedError` exhaustion circuit and the density tracker both route to the same action (emit tail gaps, stop). The density tracker subsumes the exhaustion path's job with better detection latency. One well-calibrated congestion signal should drive one stop decision; the duality is an artifact of layering, not a requirement.
5. **(Watch, not delete) Per-run `RunBudget` as a first-class governor.** Its *correctness property* — emitting `run_cap_deferred` gaps that do **not** arm the source-pressure cooldown — is essential and stays. Its existence as a separate class with a trip-reason taxonomy is borderline-incidental scaffolding that a run-level abort signal could absorb; low priority, leave it.

Note on what is **already done**: the research frames assumed PDPP still stacked *two* pre-flight waits (lane jitter + a separate `ProviderPacing.admit()`). The live code has already collapsed this — `pacingMode: "signal"`, `launchDelayHint: () => providerBudget.pacingDelayHint()`, and `max(...)`-not-sum at `adaptive-lane.ts:466`. The dual-controller stacking is **not** an open defect; the rate-AIMD already lives in `ProviderPacing` and rides as a hint. The remaining incidental complexity is the *floor that overrides that hint*, the *frozen concurrency dimension*, and the *tailStop the floor necessitates*.

## 4. How the attacks were resolved

**Attack 1 — two-dimensionality (RPM vs TPM).** *Amends the verdict.* The attack is right that raw request-count rate is insufficient if TPM is the binding quota and conversation payloads vary by 1000×. But the resolution stays single-loop: redefine the variable as **quota-unit consumption rate** — token-weighted interval — not raw request rate. One variable, one signal, one loop; only the unit of measurement changes. *Amendment adopted:* the SLVP control variable is "quota-unit-adjusted rate," and where the 429 body names its axis (`model_request_limit` vs `model_token_limit`) the controller should fold payload size into the consumed-units accounting. **Where >95% confidence fails:** we have not characterized whether ChatGPT's consumer-account 429s name their axis. If opaque, single-variable request-rate is the best achievable and we accept residual oscillation when the conversation-size mix shifts — scope this honestly as "RPM-dominant unless TPM signal is legible." This does **not** reopen concurrency: in-flight parallelism is still fixed at 1, so the second dimension is payload-volume-rate, not concurrency.

**Attack 2 — no-signal cold start / probe-into-pressure is the harm (the account-risk crux).** *Amends the verdict; this is the one the owner must hear resolved explicitly.* **Position taken: fastest-safe means probe-upward-*cautiously* inside a hard ceiling — NOT conservative-with-downward-only-adaptation.** Defense: the conservative-fixed-rate steelman collapses under two facts. (a) *The ceiling drifts* — ChatGPT's effective quota moves with time-of-day, account age, and policy; a rate frozen at last week's safe maximum is either leaving throughput on the table or, after a quota cut, silently probing the flagging threshold without knowing it. Only downward adaptation catches the latter, and downward adaptation requires observing 429s — the *same* signal AIMD uses. So "fixed rate + downward-only" and "AIMD with a tiny increase constant" are the *same system*; the dichotomy is false. (b) *Irreversibility lives in high-rate overshoots and 429-bursts, not in single incremental steps.* The ban trigger is a pattern — sustained aggression, parallelism, many-429s-in-a-window — and the three essential components (conservative increment + sharp multiplicative back-off + hard ceiling *below the estimated behavioral threshold*) are precisely engineered to make the overshoot shallow and the recovery immediate. **The decisive point for PDPP:** the current architecture is neither the cautious-AIMD the ideal proposes nor the downward-only-with-fixed-ceiling the steelman would vindicate — it is a *fixed-rate system with a 1500ms floor* that forbids upward probing entirely, dressed in adaptive machinery that the floor disables. The steelman cannot justify a floor that blocks even gradual recovery after a period of clean operation at reduced rate. *Amendment adopted:* the additive-increase constant is calibrated for asymmetric irreversible downside — small enough that the controller never reaches the ceiling by surprise within a single run — and the hard ceiling is set below the *behavioral* threshold, not the *429* threshold. The AIMD structure is correct; the parameterization (and the deletion of the floor) is the fix.

**Attack 3 — latency-vs-error signal / neko observability.** *Amends the signal definition; does not touch the architecture.* The attack correctly exposes that "429 and/or latency" borrows latency from systems (Scrapy, Envoy) with clean HTTP-header RTT, whereas the neko path observes end-to-end browser wall time confounded by DOM rendering, JS execution, container CPU, and screenshot latency — likely too noisy to use as a graduated Vegas-style signal. It also surfaces a signal class the research omitted: **behavioral challenges** (CAPTCHA, re-auth, empty-where-non-empty content) that fire *before* 429 and *are* observable through the browser. *Amendment adopted:* replace the flat "429 and/or latency" with a tiered signal — (1) behavioral-challenge → small multiplicative decrease *and* route to the retry/session-health layer (session-trust event, not mere backoff); (2) latency deviation → advisory only, gated on an unmeasured neko noise floor; (3) 429 → definitive full multiplicative decrease. Honest characterization: the candidate is **"Reno-style with a ban-risk ceiling,"** strictly better than today's floored implementation; the **"Vegas-style with domain-specific early signals"** is the right long-term target *if and only if* neko latency variance can be characterized empirically. The architecture (one rate variable, AIMD, hard ceiling, separate retry) survives all three attacks intact.

## 5. The implementation delta (sized)

Pre-condition already met: dual-wait stacking is collapsed (`max(...)`, `pacingMode: "signal"`, `launchDelayHint → pacingDelayHint()`). The delta is therefore *smaller* than the frames assumed — it removes the floor that defeats the already-correct hint.

1. **Dissolve the jitter floor so the rate-AIMD binds.** In `connectors/chatgpt/index.ts`: lower `CONVO_DETAIL_PAUSE_MIN_MS` from `1500` (and `MAX` from `3000`) to a sub-second pattern-jitter (e.g. 50–250ms) or 0, and move the *real* lower bound into `ProviderPacing`'s `minIntervalMs` (an owner knob). Net effect: `launchWaitMs = max(launchDelay≈jitter, cooldown, pacingDelayHint())` becomes governed by `pacingDelayHint()` — the GCRA rate-AIMD — instead of being floored at 1500ms. **~2 constants + 1 knob plumbed; ~20 LOC.**

2. **Confirm the signal pipeline = ProviderPacing rate-AIMD as sole velocity source.** `recordSuccess()` (`index.ts:2638`) shaves the interval toward `minIntervalMs`; `recordThrottle({retryAfterAlreadySlept})` (`index.ts:2571`) multiplies it up. Tune `additiveIncreaseMs` *down* (Attack 2: full recovery spans multiple runs, not requests) and keep `multiplicativeDecreaseFactor` sharp. **Knob re-tuning, ~0 structural LOC.**

3. **Launch-jitter floor becomes pattern-avoidance only.** The lane's `minDelayMs/maxDelayMs` (`index.ts:2389–2392`) carry desync jitter, not throttle. The throttle floor is `ProviderPacing.minIntervalMs`. **Wiring change, ~10 LOC.**

4. **Concurrency stays a hard, non-adaptive ceiling of 1.** No change to the value; optionally annotate `maybeIncreaseConcurrency`/`decreaseConcurrency` as inert under `maxConcurrency===1`, or guard them off. Do **not** build a concurrency controller. **0–5 LOC.**

5. **Replace the self-terminating recovery exit with drain-within-budget.** The `tailStopController.abort()` (`index.ts:599`) exists because the floor made tail iteration expensive. With the floor gone, replace abort-after-gap with a normal drain bounded by the existing `RunBudget`/`tailGapBound`: iterate the tail at sub-ms overhead, emit `DETAIL_GAP` for each, stop when the gap-bound or wall budget trips — no separate AbortController needed. **~30–40 LOC removed/simplified.**

6. **Signal pipeline → tiered (Attack 3), gated on measurement.** Add a behavioral-challenge detector (CAPTCHA/re-auth/empty-content) feeding a small multiplicative decrease *plus* a session-health event in the retry/governor layer; leave latency advisory-only behind an off-by-default flag until neko noise is characterized. **New detector ~40–60 LOC, flag-gated; do not ship the latency arm until measured.**

7. **(If Attack 1 is legible) token-weight the consumed units.** If the 429 body names its axis, weight `recordRequest`/pacing accounting by payload tokens. **Deferred until the 429 body is characterized.**

Pipeline summary: *neko fetch → retry layer (Retry-After, absorbedByRequestWait) → on result: recordSuccess|recordThrottle on ProviderPacing → next launch waits max(jitter, cooldown, pacingDelayHint()) with the GCRA rate-AIMD now binding → tail/over-budget → reason-discriminated DETAIL_GAP → cross-run cooldown reads upstream_pressure only.*

## 6. What remains genuinely owner's-call

1. **The safety-ceiling VALUE.** `ProviderPacing.minIntervalMs` (fastest allowed interval) and the conviction that `maxConcurrency` stays `1` are risk-tolerance settings, not research outputs. Frame 4 is unambiguous: the ceiling is *behavioral, not technical*, cumulative and stateful (unlike a memoryless token bucket), and **cannot be discovered by probing without risking the account**. The operator sets it below the *estimated flagging threshold*, inductively, from observed healthy-session behavior — the one place the system must be a fixed prior, not adaptive.
2. **The additive-increase constant.** "Slow enough that recovery spans multiple runs" is a shape the research fixes; the exact value is an owner judgment trading throughput against ban-risk margin.
3. **<95%-confidence empirical gaps the research could not settle:** (a) whether neko wall-time is clean enough to ever promote latency from advisory to graduated signal (Attack 3 — needs a measured noise floor); (b) whether ChatGPT 429 bodies name their quota axis well enough to make the variable token-weighted vs request-weighted (Attack 1 — needs response-body characterization). Until measured, ship Reno-style (429-primary, request-weighted, latency advisory-off) and scope the doc honestly as such.
4. **Whether to keep `RunBudget` as a class** or collapse it to a run-level abort signal — a maintainability preference, not a correctness question; its reason-discrimination property is non-negotiable either way.
