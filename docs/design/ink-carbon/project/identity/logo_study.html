PDPP · IDENTITY STUDY · 0.1

A mark for the grant.

PLATES
I — V · 14 studies

A brand mark should carry the idea on its back. PDPP's idea: a grant is a seam between a warm interior and a cool exterior — an object that stays whole across that boundary. Every study below is one attempt to draw that seam.

I
PRIMARY MARK · "THE SEAM"
A letterform split on the thermal axis.
I.1 — the seam, positive primary · light surface
I.2 — the seam, night primary · dark surface
IDEA

The uppercase P holds two shapes: a bowl that opens toward a person, and a descending stem that roots it in a server. The thermal seam runs vertically through the counter's interior, splitting the letter into its two halves without dividing its silhouette.

The mark is whole from a distance. The seam rewards a closer look.

RULES
  1. The counter is always r = 9% × mark width.
  2. The seam never moves off the optical vertical.
  3. Warm side is holder, cool side is issuer.
  4. Never outline. Never center-align beneath a wordmark; always hang left.

HONEST APPRAISAL

Plate I is the answer. The split-P carries the idea without forcing the viewer to decode it — it reads as a letterform first, a concept second. Plate III.3 (the channel) is the strongest ancillary; it would make a good favicon or app-icon corner mark where the P is too literal.

What I'd hand to a professional to refine: the bowl's curve (slightly too geometric, wants a hairline optical correction where it meets the stem), the counter's vertical position (should drift up ~2 units for optical centering in the upper bowl), and the thermal seam's exact hue ramp.

WHAT THIS ISN'T

Not a finished identity. A studio would spend three weeks refining Plate I, draw a custom wordmark to replace the Fraunces-borrowed pdpp, test it on business cards and favicons at real size, and throw out at least one of the Plate III variants. This study is a credible first draft — honest geometry with a real idea underneath.
