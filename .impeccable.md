# Design Context

## Target audience
The PDPP leadership build team: a CEO who will show this to founders and standards bodies (Linux Foundation), a head of product evaluating migration from current architecture, and engineers evaluating protocol adoption. Secondary: GTM team creating content, standards reviewers auditing rigor.

## Use cases
1. CEO opens the URL in a meeting and the room's first reaction is "this looks serious"
2. An engineer scrolls through and understands the protocol's flow, then drills into spec-level depth
3. The head of product sees the consent UX and realizes it's better than what they currently offer
4. Someone records a narrated walkthrough video over the reference

## Brand personality/tone
**Linear/Plaid/Stripe meets OAuth.** Technically precise. Visually restrained but with perfect craft. Dense information presented with exceptional spacing and hierarchy. Monospace where it means protocol data. Every detail signals world-class engineering.

Not academic. Not startup-flashy. Not consumer-warm. Serious tools built by people who care about both correctness and beauty.

## Aesthetic direction
**Editorial minimalism with protocol precision.** Think Stripe's documentation density meets the Illustrated TLS 1.3's pedagogical rigor. Light mode. Generous whitespace that breathes. A single accent system: warm copper for human/consent surfaces, cool blue for protocol/system surfaces. The temperature duality IS the brand.

The one thing someone will remember: the field projection moment — 8 fields enter the server, 4 come out the other side, and you viscerally understand what "parameterized consent" means.

## Technical constraints
- Next.js App Router (public site: apps/site; operator console: apps/console), Tailwind v4, shadcn base-nova, Fumadocs for /docs
- Geist (sans) + JetBrains Mono (mono)
- OKLCH color system with semantic tokens
- Only animate transform and opacity (GPU-composited)
- 2-level progressive disclosure max per NN/g research
- The page must work as a scroll-through presentation AND a deep-dive reference

## What this is NOT
- Not a dashboard
- Not a marketing page with stock photos
- Not a three-panel developer tool
- Not dark mode with glowing accents
